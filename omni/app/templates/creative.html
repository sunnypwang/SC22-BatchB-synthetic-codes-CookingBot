NanoBytes

An AI designed to help you reduce food waste, and make the most out of your ingredients.

Your New Cooking Companion

Description

Our AI generates recipes based upon the ingredients that the user inputs. For example, if you enter mushrooms and chicken into our model, it will generate a recipe that includes these two ingredients. Users can specify filters and categories for their recipe by picking from multiple drop-down menus. This system can help reduce food waste and find more food options for those with specific likes and needs with what they have available.

Get Started!





Specs

We used CoCalc Server, Google Colab, NLP, Flask, HTML, aitextgen, CSS, Python, and JavaScript. We used GPT-2 as our main model. GPT-2 is a training model out of the models provided. GPT-2 worked the best out of the models provided, so we used GPT-2.

Process

Step 1: Finding Data
We found a dataset on Kaggle that included 500,000 recipes from food.com with 300 categories.
Step 2: Pre-processing
The Back-End team sorted data and deleted unneeded columns (AuthorID, AuthorName, etc.), cutting the data to 30,000. The rest of the data was sorted and filtered into categories.
Step 3: Training
Downloaded and trained for the first time with 40,000 steps, cut the data to 50,000, sorting and filtering categories. Repeated with 50,000 instead of 30,000 and trained 66,000 steps in the AI.
Step 4: Website design
To design our website, we used a theme on Start Bootstrap that was similar to our intended design, so we adapted it to include all of our needed elements.
Step 5: Setting up the website
Making sure all the categories were included on the input page and reducing selection options on the drop down menu, which was a struggle and having an ingredient textbox. Linking buttons and options together.
Step 6: Final Corrections and Presentation
We added finishing touches, ensured our model's functionality, and formatted our website to look presentable.
